## Definition

[NOD_DEF] is the one-sentence description property attached to a [NODE_ID]. It is the human-readable definitional gloss for the node, distinct from [NODE_NAM] (the node's name) and [NODE_TAG] (the node's short tag).

## Rules

- A [NOD_DEF] is a single declarative sentence in canonical English describing the node identified by a [NODE_ID].
- A [NOD_DEF] is the value carried in the Definition column of the [FROID_NS] index for the corresponding [NODE_ID].
- A [NOD_DEF] MAY be empty for nodes whose definition is implicit in surrounding context; it is the only [NODE_ID]-property that admits absence.

## Constraints

- A [NODE_ID] has at most one [NOD_DEF] per assignment. [NOD_DEF] supersession follows the same lifecycle as the parent [FOLIO]; revisions of definition prose belong in revision-set artifacts, not in independent [NOD_DEF] folios.

## Relations

- [NOD_DEF] is a child of [NODE_ID].
- [NOD_DEF] is a peer of [NODE_NAM] and [NODE_TAG] under [NODE_ID]; the three are the human-readable property layer attached to a [NODE_ID].

## Definition

[FLIO_SUP] is the supersession field-pair concept in [FLIO_REF]. It groups two directional fields, [FLO_SPUP] and [FLO_SPDN], that together model the supersession relation between [FOLIO].

[FLO_SPUP] (`flo_spup`) carries a list of [NODE_ID] that this [FOLIO] supersedes — the upstream direction.

[FLO_SPDN] (`flo_spdn`) carries a list of [NODE_ID] that supersede this [FOLIO] — the downstream direction.

The two fields are reciprocal: if A.`flo_spup` contains B, then B.`flo_spdn` MUST contain A.

## Rules

- `flo_spup` and `flo_spdn`, when present, are lists of [NODE_ID].
- Each [NODE_ID] in either list MUST resolve to an existing [FOLIO] in [FROID_NS].
- Reciprocity: if A.`flo_spup` contains B, then B.`flo_spdn` MUST contain A.
- A [NODE_ID] MUST NOT appear in both `flo_spup` and `flo_spdn` of the same [FLIO_REF].

## Constraints

- The two fields' second-layer roles ([FLO_SPUP] and [FLO_SPDN]) are direction concepts rather than enumerated values; they are themselves manifest-field concepts at Layer 2 of the three-layer pattern.

## Relations

- [FLIO_SUP] is recorded by [FLIO_REF] via the field pair `flo_spup` and `flo_spdn`.
- [FLIO_SUP]'s direction concepts are [FLO_SPUP] and [FLO_SPDN].
- [FLIO_SUP] references zero or more [FOLIO] in either direction.

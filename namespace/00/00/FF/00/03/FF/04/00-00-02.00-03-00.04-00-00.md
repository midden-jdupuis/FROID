## Rules — Structural Binding

- A `structural` [FOLIO] is valid iff there is one deepest non-zero position and every higher non-root position above it is also non-zero. Every lower position below the node's depth MUST be `00`. Gaps are invalid.
- A non-root `structural` [FOLIO] requires full ancestor chain: every `structural` identity node on its ancestor chain to the root MUST also be assigned.
- If a non-root `structural` [FOLIO] is assigned, every `structural` identity node on its ancestor chain to the root MUST also be assigned.
- `structural` [FOLIO] have parent and child relations in [FROID_NS] derived from their [NODE_ID].

## Rules — Overlay Binding

- An `overlay` [FOLIO] is valid iff MM is one of `{01, 02, 03}`, TT is non-identity, and II is non-identity. The selector position VV, BB, CC, UU, and KK MAY each be `00` or non-zero independently.
- An `overlay` [FOLIO] whose selector position are all `00` is valid and applies at global scope within its MM and TT.
- `overlay` [FOLIO] have no parent or child relation in [FROID_NS].
- For one `overlay` lineage within one MM, selector, and TT, a later II MUST list the immediately prior same-lineage [FOLIO] in `supersedes`.
- Peer `overlay` [FOLIO] across MM=01, MM=02, and MM=03 with the same selector, TT, and II form one revision set. One revision set MUST materialize all three peers.

## Rules — Binding Mechanics

- A [FOLIO] with `binding: structural` is one whose position in [FROID_NS] is part of its identity. Its meaning is entangled with its parent; its descendants' meanings are entangled with its own. The folio cannot be moved without changing what it means. Structural folios form the primary tree hierarchy.
- A [FOLIO] with `binding: overlay` is one whose position in [FROID_NS] is a filing decision. Its meaning is intrinsic; moving the folio would not change what it means. The tree holds it; the tree does not define it. Overlay folios apply by reference and do not form a hierarchical structure.

## Rules — Design Intent for Vocabulary and Concept Folios

- Vocabulary folios — folios that define enumerated values or vocabulary terms (such as NORMATIVE, DESCRIPTIVE as values of authority) — MUST use `binding: overlay` when created. These folios are definitional anchors applied by reference; their meaning is intrinsic and does not depend on structural position.
- Concept folios — folios that define concepts referenced from manifests (such as AUTH and BIND, the definitions of manifest fields) — MUST use `binding: overlay` when created. These concepts apply namespace-wide by citation, not by tree descent; their meaning is drawn from their role as referenced authorities, not from position in the tree.

## Constraints

- `binding` field MUST be one of `overlay` or `structural`.
- The root [FOLIO] (`00.00.00.00.00.00.00.00.00`) has `binding=structural` and has no parent.
- Once assigned, a binding type is immutable (`binding=structural` or `binding=overlay` cannot change).

## Relations

- [FLIO_BND] defines the binding semantics of [FOLIO].
- [FLIO_BND] is a child of [FOLIO].
- Structural-bound [FOLIO] form the primary hierarchical tree.
- Overlay-bound [FOLIO] form scoped rule overlays.

## Notes

- Storage structure does not define node existence. Directory presence in the filesystem back end does not constitute a [FOLIO].
- Parent and child relations are logical relations derived from [NODE_ID], not from storage layout.
- Overlay [FOLIO] naturally organize into three semantic roles (not formal structural distinctions): vocabulary folios (enumerated values), concept folios (cited definitions), and meta folios (cross-cutting records and commentary). These roles are semantic; all overlay folios share the same binding mechanics regardless of their role.
- "Meta" names a semantic subcategory of overlay folios, not a binding type. It captures folios that serve higher-order functions (ADRs, decision logs, bug lists, design retrospectives). If the meta role becomes load-bearing (needs formal tracking), it should be implemented as an optional manifest attribute, not as a new binding value.

- Vocabulary update (2026-05-02): `feature` → `structural`, `standalone` → `overlay`. The substantive rules are unchanged; only the names of the binding values have moved.

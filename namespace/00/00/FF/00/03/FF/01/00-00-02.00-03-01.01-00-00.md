```
node_id: 00-00-02.00-03-01.01-00-00
node_nam: Folio Manifest
node_tag: FLIO_REF
```

## Rules

- The [FLIO_REF] filename and the `node_id` field identify the same [FOLIO] and MUST match exactly.
- A manifest whose filename and `node_id` field do not match is invalid.
- If multiple [FLIO_REF] records claim the same assigned [NODE_ID], that [FROID_NS] state is invalid.
- Once a [FOLIO] is committed, its [FLIO_REF] content is frozen and MUST NOT be edited or re-serialized in place.
- `node_nam` MUST be 1 to 128 printable ASCII characters (U+0020 to U+007E), MUST NOT begin or end with whitespace.
- `flio_crt` is the [NODE_ID] of the date [FOLIO] for the commit date of this [FOLIO]. It resolves by reference per [FLIO_CRT].
- `flo_bind` MUST be one of `overlay` or `structural`.
- `status`, if present, MUST be one of `active`, `superseded`, `deferred`, or `retired`. Status does not transition in place; a later state requires a new [FOLIO].
- `flo_auth` MUST be present and MUST be one of [FL_NORM] or [FL_DESC].
- Manifest-field concepts follow a three-layer pattern: Layer 1 (structural) is the field-in-manifest (e.g., `flo_bind`) — structurally part of [FLIO_REF], constitutive of the manifest. Layer 2 (overlay) is the concept definition (e.g., [FLO_BIND]) — the cited definition of what the field means. Layer 3 (overlay) is enumerated values (e.g., FL_STRCT, FL_OVRLY) — the legal values the field can take. Each layer cites the next; each layer's binding follows from its role.
- `flio_own` is the [NODE_ID] of the owner [FOLIO] for this [FOLIO]. It resolves by reference per [FLIO_OWN].
- A manifest field whose value is a [NODE_ID] (`flio_own`, `flio_crt`, `flo_spup`, `flo_spdn`) MUST resolve to an existing [FOLIO] in [FROID_NS] at commit time.
- A `flio_pld` entry is one artifact reference string. In the filesystem back end, it is the artifact filename in the same leaf directory and MUST NOT equal `{NODE_ID}.frd`.
- `flio_pld` is an ordered list. Two payload entries in the same [FLIO_REF] MUST NOT reference the same artifact.
- [FL_HSCOR] is a 64-character lowercase hexadecimal SHA-256 digest computed over the committed [FLIO_REF] manifest fields, excluding [FL_HSCOR], [FL_HSPLD], and [FL_HSSPR] from the digest input. [FL_HSCOR] is present on every [FLIO_REF] regardless of payload.
- [FL_HSPLD], if present, is a 64-character lowercase hexadecimal SHA-256 digest computed over payload entries in order at time of commit. [FL_HSPLD] is required when `flio_pld` is non-empty; it MUST be omitted when `flio_pld` is omitted or empty.
- [FL_HSSPR] is a 64-character lowercase hexadecimal SHA-256 digest computed and appended at supersession time, chaining from [FL_HSCOR] and [FL_HSPLD] (if present) plus supersession metadata (replaced NODE_ID and timestamp). Once [FL_HSSPR] is appended, no further write or supersession events are permitted on the [FOLIO], and it is closed.
- `flo_spup` and `flo_spdn` are lists of [NODE_ID] governed by [FLIO_SUP], with [FLO_SPUP] (upstream) and [FLO_SPDN] (downstream) as the directional concepts.
- A [NODE_ID] MUST NOT appear in both `flo_spup` and `flo_spdn` of the same manifest.
- Reciprocity: if A.`flo_spup` contains B, then B.`flo_spdn` MUST contain A. The rule applies whenever both [FOLIO] are present in [FROID_NS].

## Constraints

- `TT=02` is an optional companion to `TT=01`. For one selector, MM, and II, at most one `TT=02` [FOLIO] may exist. If a `TT=02` [FOLIO] exists, the corresponding `TT=01` MUST also exist.

## Relations

- [FLIO_REF] describes exactly one [FOLIO].
- [FLIO_REF] is required by [FOLIO]; a valid [NODE_ID] position with no [FLIO_REF] is unassigned.
- [FLIO_REF] references zero or more [FLIO_ART] via `flio_pld`.

## Notes

- A minimal [FOLIO] contains only its [FLIO_REF]. [FLIO_ART] are optional.
- `flo_bind=structural` means the [FOLIO] follows the ordinary full position tree and derives meaning from its position. `flo_bind=overlay` means it is a scoped rule or reference artifact whose meaning is intrinsic.

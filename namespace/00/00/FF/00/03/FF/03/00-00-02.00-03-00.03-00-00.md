## Rules

- [FLIO_AUT] is present on every [FLIO_REF] and is required. Every [FOLIO] must declare its authority stance.
- [FLIO_AUT] MUST be one of `normative` or `descriptive`. No other values are valid at [FROID_NS] 1.0.
- `normative` means the [FOLIO] governs behavior and enforces rules. Consuming tools, validators, and rule engines MUST comply with normative assertions.
- `descriptive` means the [FOLIO] provides context, explanation, or commentary. Consuming tools MAY use it for documentation, rationale, or cross-reference lookup; they are not required to enforce it.
- [FLIO_AUT] and [FLIO_BND] are orthogonal dimensions. A [FOLIO] can be any combination of authority and binding type.
- The four cells of the AUT × BND matrix are: normative+structural (rules that scope downward through tree structure), normative+overlay (rules that apply by reference), descriptive+structural (positioned explanation whose meaning depends on parent), and descriptive+overlay (unbound explanation or commentary).
- [FLIO_AUT] is independent from [FLIO_BND]. The authority of a [FOLIO] does not determine or constrain its binding type, and vice versa.
- A future version MAY introduce a third authority value (e.g., to represent "recommended but not binding" or informational subcategories). The two-value vocabulary at 1.0 is complete and sufficient; future additions will be explicitly versioned.

## Constraints

- `authority` field MUST be present in every [FLIO_REF].
- `authority` MUST be one of `normative` or `descriptive`; no other values are permitted.
- No [FOLIO] may change its authority value after commitment. Authority is immutable once the [FOLIO] is created.

## Relations

- [FLIO_AUT] is a child of [FOLIO].
- [FLIO_AUT] is paired with [FLIO_BND] on every [FLIO_REF] as orthogonal governing properties.
- [FLIO_AUT] references [AUTH] as its concept definition (when [AUTH] folios exist).

## Notes

- The four-cell matrix clarifies that authority (normative/descriptive) and binding (structural/overlay) are independent governance dimensions, not coupled decisions.
- Normative folios are load-bearing and affect system semantics. Descriptive folios are context and may be omitted without breaking the system.

- Vocabulary update (2026-05-02): `feature` → `structural`, `standalone` → `overlay`. The substance of the AUT × BND orthogonality is unchanged; only the names of the BND values have moved.
- The orthogonality of AUT and BND is what makes the four-cell model work and prevents false constraints on folio design.

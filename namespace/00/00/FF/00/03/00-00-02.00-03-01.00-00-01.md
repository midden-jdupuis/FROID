```
node_id: 00-00-02.00-03-01.00-00-01
node_nam: FROID Node
node_tag: FOLIO
```

## Rules

- A [FOLIO] exists at a [NODE_ID] if and only if a [FLIO_REF] is present at that coordinate. Filesystem presence without a [FLIO_REF] is not a [FOLIO] and must not be inferred as one.
- A valid [NODE_ID] position in [FROID_NS] does not by itself imply that a [FOLIO] exists there. Unassigned positions have no [FOLIO] and no [FLIO_REF].
- Once committed to FROID, a [FOLIO] is immutable. Its [NODE_ID], [FLIO_REF], and any associated [ARTIFACT] are frozen. A later state is represented by a different [FOLIO].
- A `structural` [FOLIO] is valid iff there is one deepest non-zero position and every higher non-root position above it is also non-zero. Every lower position below the node's depth MUST be `00`. Gaps are invalid.
- A `overlay` [FOLIO] is valid iff MM is one of `{01, 02, 03}`, TT is non-identity, and II is non-identity. The selector position VV, BB, CC, UU, and KK MAY each be `00` or non-zero independently.
- A `overlay` [FOLIO] whose selector position are all `00` is valid and applies at global scope within its MM and TT.
- `overlay` [FOLIO] have no parent or child relation in [FROID_NS].
- The root [FOLIO] (`00.00.00.00.00.00.00.00.00`) has `binding=structural` and has no parent.
- If a non-root `structural` [FOLIO] is assigned, every `structural` identity node on its ancestor chain to the root MUST also be assigned.
- Peer `overlay` [FOLIO] across MM=01, MM=02, and MM=03 with the same selector, TT, and II form one revision set. One revision set MUST materialize all three peers.
- For one `overlay` lineage within one MM, selector, and TT, a later II MUST list the immediately prior same-lineage [FOLIO] in `supersedes`.
- A [NODE] is the immutable abstract concept identified by a [NODE_ID]. A [FOLIO] is the concrete projection of a [NODE] into a back end (filesystem or database). [NODE]-level immutability is permanent; [FOLIO]-level changes occur only through defined commit and supersession events.
- A [FOLIO] payload MAY contain local artifact filenames or [NODE_ID] references to other [FOLIO] instances, linking them as dependencies or related content.
- A [NODE] is described by projections at ([Mode], [Type]) coordinates. Both the Mode and Type axes are open and extensible; new Mode and Type values may be introduced without constraint.
- All projections of a [NODE] must be mutually consistent in their representation of node identity and state. Where [TYP_M1T1] is present, it is authoritative for resolving conflicts between projections.
- [TYP_M1T1] is required for every [FOLIO] with payload; it serves as the primary human-readable entry point and source of canonical state.

## Constraints

- `00` in a [SEGMENT] denotes the identity position at its position.
- `FF` is reserved and MUST NOT appear in an assigned [NODE_ID].
- The root [FOLIO] MUST be assigned if any [FOLIO] is assigned in [FROID_NS].

## Relations

- [FOLIO] is identified by one [NODE_ID].
- [FOLIO] is described by one [FLIO_REF].
- [FOLIO] MAY reference zero or more [ARTIFACT].
- The parent of a non-root `structural` [FOLIO] is the [FOLIO] whose [NODE_ID] equals the child's [NODE_ID] with the deepest non-zero [SEGMENT] replaced by `00`.
- Two [FOLIO] are equal iff their assigned [NODE_ID] are equal.

## Notes

- Storage structure does not define node existence. Directory presence in the filesystem back end does not constitute a [FOLIO].
- Parent and child relations are logical relations derived from [NODE_ID], not from storage layout.

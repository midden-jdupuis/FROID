```
node_id: 00-00-01.00-03-01.00-00-00
node_nam: Folio
node_tag: FOLIO
```

## Definition

[FOLIO] is a committed node in [FROID_NS].

Each [FOLIO] has exactly one assigned [NODE_ID] and exactly one [FLIO_REF]. A [FOLIO] may also be associated with zero or more [FLIO_ART].

A [FOLIO] has an [FLIO_BND]: [FL_STRCT] or [FL_OVRLY]. A [FL_STRCT] [FOLIO] participates in the primary [FROID_NS] structural tree, organized by [SEG_GRAN] segments. A [FL_OVRLY] [FOLIO] is a scoped rule artifact whose non-zero [SEG_GRAN] selector segments select the subtree to which it applies.

A minimal [FOLIO] contains only its [FLIO_REF]. [FLIO_ART] are optional. Child [FOLIO] are separate [FOLIO] with their own [NODE_ID] and [FLIO_REF].

## Rules

- A valid [NODE_ID] position in [FROID_NS] does not by itself imply that a [FOLIO] exists there. Unassigned positions have no [FOLIO] and no [FLIO_REF].
- Once committed to [FROID_NS], a [FOLIO] is immutable. Its [NODE_ID] and the contents of its [FLIO_REF] payload at commit time are frozen. The [FLIO_REF] payload list MAY grow over time as additional [FLIO_ART] are attached; existing [FLIO_ART] files MUST NOT change.
- Lifecycle transitions of a [FOLIO] occur via supersession ([FL_SUPER]), not via editing. A later state is represented by a different [FOLIO] with a different [NODE_ID].
- The root [FOLIO] (`00-00-00.00-00-00.00-00-00`) has [FLIO_BND] of [FL_STRCT] and has no parent.
- If a non-root [FL_STRCT] [FOLIO] is assigned, every [FL_STRCT] identity node on its ancestor chain to the root MUST also be assigned.

## Constraints

- `00` in a [NODE_SEG] denotes the identity position at its position.
- `FF` is reserved and MUST NOT appear in an assigned [NODE_ID].
- The root [FOLIO] MUST be assigned if any [FOLIO] is assigned in [FROID_NS].
- Storage structure does not define [FOLIO] existence. Directory presence in [FROID_NS] does not constitute a [FOLIO]; only the presence of a `{NODE_ID}.frd` file does.
- [FLIO_BND] is immutable; once assigned, [FL_STRCT] or [FL_OVRLY] cannot change.

## Relations

- [FOLIO] is identified by one [NODE_ID].
- [FOLIO] is described by one [FLIO_REF].
- [FOLIO] MAY reference zero or more [FLIO_ART] via [FLIO_PLD].
- The parent of a non-root [FL_STRCT] [FOLIO] is the [FOLIO] whose [NODE_ID] equals the child's [NODE_ID] with the deepest non-zero [SEG_GRAN] segment replaced by `00`.
- Two [FOLIO] are equal iff their assigned [NODE_ID] are equal.
- [FOLIO] may supersede or be superseded by another [FOLIO] via [FL_SUPER].
- [FOLIO] may reference other [FOLIO] via [FL_REFER].
- Parent and child relations are logical relations derived from [NODE_ID], not from storage layout.

## Notes

- [FOLIO] MAY also carry meta-artifacts at the [FOLIO]'s [NODE_ID] coordinate distinguished by reserved file extensions: `.adr`, `.bug`, `.prk`, `.map`, `.smry`. Meta-artifacts share the [FOLIO]'s [NODE_ID]; only the extension differs.

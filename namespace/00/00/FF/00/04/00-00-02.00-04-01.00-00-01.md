## Mode Semantics

- **MM=01 (Declarative)** — Atomic declarations and canonical definitions. Holds irreducible semantic units independent of justification or realization.
- **MM=02 (Governance)** — Rules, constraints, standards, and policy. Governs what is allowed, required, and means; applies to one or more [FOLIO] simultaneously.
- **MM=03 (Realization)** — Executable or materialized artifacts. Implementation, code, configuration, or deployed form.

## Rules

- MM assignable values are closed to `{01, 02, 03}`. No other non-identity values are assignable at MM.
- MM=`00` remains the mode identity node per the general identity rule.
- Every `overlay` [FOLIO] with the same selector, TT, and II MUST materialize all three mode peers (MM=01, MM=02, MM=03) as a complete revision set.

## Constraints

- MM is a controlled-vocabulary position. Its value set is fixed to `{00, 01, 02, 03}` for this revision.
- MM=`FF` is reserved and syntactically valid but MUST NOT appear in an assigned [NODE_ID].

## Artifact Type Pairing

Core types are binary and available in both modes for maximum flexibility:
- **Type 1 (TT=01)** — Markdown: [TYP_M1T1] (Mode 1) or [TYP_M2T1] (Mode 2)
- **Type 2 (TT=02)** — YAML: [TYP_M1T2] (Mode 1) or [TYP_M2T2] (Mode 2)

Canonical pairings are MM=01→Type 1 and MM=02→Type 2, but either type can be used in either mode.

## FOLIO Artifact Constraint

If a [FOLIO] has artifacts, the payload MUST include at least one **Type 1** artifact ([TYP_M1T1] or [TYP_M2T1]) as the human-readable entry point.

## Relations

- [MODE] defines the MM (mode) segment within position.
- [MODE] has three assignable child modes: [MODE_1], [MODE_2], and [MODE_3].

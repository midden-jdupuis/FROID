## Definition

[MODE_1] is the canonical declaration mode. It holds atomic, declarative seeds of intent.

A [MODE_1] artifact is: atomic, non-normative, non-derived, and semantically complete on its own. It defines what something is, independent of how it is justified or realized.

[MODE_1] can contain any atomic, declarative specification — structures, schemas, relations, rules, events, queries, or behaviors — as long as each is irreducible and self-contained.

## Artifact Types

[MODE_1] uses Type 1 artifacts:
- **[TYP_M1T1]** — Mode 1 Type 1 Markdown (canonical)
- **[TYP_M1T2]** — Mode 1 Type 2 YAML (alternative)

Type 1 (Markdown) is the canonical choice and serves as the human-readable entry point for FOLIOs with artifacts.

## Rules

- Full implementations belong in [MODE_3], not [MODE_1].
- Explanations, rationale, and history belong in [MODE_2], not [MODE_1].
- Multi-concern documents must be cleaved into separate [FOLIO] before placement in [MODE_1].
- One [NODE_ID] = one irreducible semantic unit. A [MODE_1] artifact must not combine multiple distinct declarations.
- [MODE_1] artifacts are governed by corresponding [MODE_2] artifacts (if present).
- [MODE_1] artifacts are realized by corresponding [MODE_3] artifacts (if present).

## Constraints

- A [MODE_1] artifact MUST be atomic: it cannot be decomposed into two independently meaningful declarations.
- A [MODE_1] artifact MUST NOT contain normative rules (MUST/SHALL/MAY governing others).
- A [MODE_1] artifact MUST NOT contain rationale, history, or justification.
- A [MODE_1] artifact MUST NOT contain implementation or realization content.
- [MODE_1] Type pairing: Both Type 1 (Markdown) and Type 2 (YAML) are available; Type 1 is canonical.

## Relations

- [MODE_1] is a child of [MODE] at MM=01.
- [MODE_1] artifacts are governed by corresponding [MODE_2] artifacts.
- [MODE_1] artifacts are realized by corresponding [MODE_3] artifacts.
- Every [FOLIO] with MM=01 belongs to [MODE_1] interpretation layer.
- [MODE_1] serves as the entry point (human-readable source-of-truth) for multi-mode node sets.

## Definition

[MODE] occupies the MM position and defines the content-category role of a [FOLIO].

[FROID_NS] operates with three agent modes: [MODE_1] (MM=01), [MODE_2] (MM=02), and [MODE_3] (MM=03). MM=`00` is the mode identity node.

## Rules

- MM assignable values are closed to `{01, 02, 03}`. No other non-identity values are assignable at MM.
- MM=`00` remains the mode identity node per the general identity rule.
- Every `overlay` [FOLIO] with the same selector, TT, and II MUST materialize all three mode peers (MM=01, MM=02, MM=03) as a complete revision set.
- A [FOLIO] with MM=01 is declarative and defines the thing.
- A [FOLIO] with MM=02 is governance and defines rules governing the thing.
- A [FOLIO] with MM=03 is realization and implements or materializes the thing.

## Constraints

- MM is a controlled-vocabulary position. Its value set is fixed to `{00, 01, 02, 03}` for this revision.
- MM=`FF` is reserved and syntactically valid but MUST NOT appear in an assigned [NODE_ID].
- No other position has such a closed-vocabulary constraint by default; MM is unique.
- Overlay revision sets MUST include all three MM peers; incomplete sets are invalid.

## Relations

- [MODE] is the MM position within position.
- [MODE] has three assignable child modes: [MODE_1], [MODE_2], and [MODE_3].
- [MODE] constrains every [FOLIO] by determining its interpretation layer (declarative, governance, or realization).
- [MODE] is referenced by the MM [SEGMENT] position in every [NODE_ID].
- Each [MODE_1] may be accompanied by corresponding [MODE_2] and [MODE_3] peers for the same selector and TT.

## Definition

A [NODE_BLK] is the unit of scoping in [FROID_NS]. Each block consists of a fixed sequence of [NODE_SEG] resolving to a document via locating segments and to a field on that document via a terminating classifying segment. A [NODE_ID] is composed of an ordered sequence of [NODE_BLK] whose count is set per-projection.

## Rules

- A [NODE_BLK] is composed of [SEG_GRAN] segments followed by exactly one [SEG_BINN] segment.
- The [SEG_BINN] segment of a [NODE_BLK] is always the deepest segment of that block. Field-terminality forces this: a field is the terminal element of a document and admits no sub-resolution; the segment that classifies the field must therefore terminate the block.
- The [SEG_GRAN] segments of a [NODE_BLK] locate a document within the parent scope. The [SEG_BINN] segment classifies a field on that document.
- Block 1 (WHO) is framework-fixed across all [PROJECT]: VV (position 1, company — framework-fixed role) / BB (position 2, branch — tenant-internal, the company allocates its branches) / MM (position 3, [SEG_BINN] classifying against [MODE]).
- Block 2 (WHAT NAMESPACE) is framework-fixed across all [PROJECT]: RR (position 4, namespace instantiation — tenant-allocated, selects which [PROJECT] applies) / CC (position 5, projection-id — equals the [PROJECT]'s catalog-row id) / TT (position 6, [SEG_BINN] classifying against [TYPE]).
- Blocks 3..N are [PROJECT]-authored: the [PROJECT] specifies the [SEG_GRAN] roles, the [SEG_BINN] classification axis, and the block count.
- The framework specifies the abstract pattern (locating segments + terminating bin per block) and the segment functions of blocks 1 and 2. The [PROJECT] specifies blocks 3..N.
- The [NODE_BLK] count of a [FROID_NS] instantiation is fixed at namespace creation and recorded in the [FROID_NS] root [FOLIO]. Different [PROJECT] declare different block counts.
- [NODE_BLK] supersedes the prior [NODE_BLK] [FOLIO] formerly at `00-00-00.00-01-00.04-00-00`, which framed [NODE_BLK] as a structural grouping mechanism. The prior [FOLIO] was a one-line stub with no body; deleted 2026-05-05 (Option A) without formal supersession ceremony since there was no body to preserve.

## Constraints

- A [NODE_BLK] MUST contain at least two [SEG_GRAN] and exactly one [SEG_BINN].
- The [SEG_BINN] segment MUST be the last segment of the [NODE_BLK].
- [PROJ_FRD] (the framework projection at CC=01) instantiates three [NODE_BLK] of three segments each: Block 1 (WHO) = (VV, BB, MM); Block 2 (WHAT NAMESPACE) = (RR, CC, TT); Block 3 (WHAT ELEMENT) = (UU, KK, II). Block 3's segment roles (UU=unit, KK=callable, II=internal-bin) are [PROJ_FRD]'s choice; other [PROJECT] declare different block-3 segments and may declare more blocks (e.g., [PROJ_TIM] uses 4 [NODE_BLK]).
- [NODE_BLK] specifications are not portable across [FROID_NS] projections by default. Some specifications (mode block, type block) are domain-agnostic and portable; others (unit/callable, etc.) are domain-specific. A portable-block library is reserved for v2.0+ and not specified here.

## Relations

- [NODE_BLK] is a child of [FROID_NS].
- [NODE_BLK] is composed of [NODE_SEG].
- [NODE_BLK] enables [SEG_GRAN] and [SEG_BINN] to be classified within a local scope.
- [NODE_BLK] is the carrier of block-scoped governance: rules, authority, naming, and supersession all admit block-scope in addition to per-[FOLIO] and namespace-wide scopes.
- [NODE_BLK] facets are decomposed into [BLK_PATN] (pattern), [BLK_SCPE] (scoping function), and [BLK_COMP] (composition function).

## Notes

- The block pattern (locating-segments + terminating-bin) is derived from the principle that fields are terminal, not asserted as a stylistic rule. This derivation is the load-bearing observation of the [NODE_BLK] specification.
- Block-scoped authority and use-class governance are independent dimensions that compose. A user holding authority over a block-scope and a use-class declared on that same block-scope are governed by separate rules. v1.0 names this future seam without resolving it; an explicit authority × use-class composition rule is reserved for the authority operationalization round.
- A [NODE_BLK] whose [SEG_GRAN]/[SEG_BINN] specifications are publicly published while its [MOD_1] / [MOD_3] content remains private is termed a "compiled block." Compiled-block packaging supports cross-organizational coordination (public contracts, private content) and is reserved for v2.0+. The substrate accommodates this structurally; tooling is not built in this round.

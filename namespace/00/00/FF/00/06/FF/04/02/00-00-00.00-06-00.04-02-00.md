## Definition

[SEG_BINN] classifies a [NODE_SEG] in a [NODE_BLK] as a **bin segment** — the terminating classifying segment of its block. Each [SEG_BINN] segment classifies a kind of field on the document located by the preceding [SEG_GRAN] segments.

## Rules

- Every [NODE_BLK] terminates in exactly one [SEG_BINN] segment.
- Field-terminality forces this structure: a field admits no sub-resolution, so the segment that classifies the field MUST terminate the block.
- [SEG_BINN] segments project as `FF` in the directory tree at their block boundary and do not extend the leaf directory path. The actual bin value lives in the artifact filename, not in directory structure.

## Constraints

- A [NODE_BLK] MAY NOT contain more than one [SEG_BINN] segment.
- A [NODE_BLK] MAY NOT terminate in a [SEG_GRAN] segment; the terminating segment MUST be a [SEG_BINN].
- [SEG_BINN] segments at framework-fixed positions carry framework-specified classification axes: MM (position 3) classifies against [MODE]; TT (position 6) classifies against [TYPE]. [SEG_BINN] segments in project-authored blocks receive their classification axis from the [PROJECT] in effect.

## Relations

- [SEG_BINN] is a child of [NODE_SEG].
- [SEG_BINN] is paired with [SEG_GRAN] as the two role-classifications of [NODE_SEG] within a [NODE_BLK].
- [SEG_BINN] composes with [SEG_GRAN] under [BLK_PATN] to form a [NODE_BLK].

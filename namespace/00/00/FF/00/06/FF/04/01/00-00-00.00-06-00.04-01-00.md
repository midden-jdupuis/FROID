## Definition

[SEG_GRAN] classifies a [NODE_SEG] in a [NODE_BLK] as a **granular segment** — one of the locating segments that resolves a document within the parent scope of its block.

## Rules

- A [NODE_BLK] is composed of one or more [SEG_GRAN] segments followed by exactly one [SEG_BINN] segment.
- Every [SEG_GRAN] segment locates within the parent scope of its [NODE_BLK].
- A leaf directory in [FROID_NS] terminates at the deepest non-`00` [SEG_GRAN] segment of a [NODE_ID]. [SEG_BINN] segments do not extend the leaf path; they appear in artifact filenames at the leaf.
- Intermediate `00` [SEG_GRAN] segments (between non-`00` ancestors and non-`00` descendants) are NOT elided from the path; trailing-zero folding only collapses zeros at the end of the granular sequence.

## Constraints

- A [NODE_BLK] MUST contain at least one [SEG_GRAN] segment (and in practice always begins with two; the gran/gran/bin pattern is the framework default).
- [SEG_GRAN] segments at framework-fixed positions carry framework-specified roles: VV (position 1, company), RR (position 4, instantiation), CC (position 5, projection-id). [SEG_GRAN] segments at project-authored positions (UU and beyond) receive their role from the [PROJECT] in effect.

## Relations

- [SEG_GRAN] is a child of [NODE_SEG].
- [SEG_GRAN] is paired with [SEG_BINN] as the two role-classifications of [NODE_SEG] within a [NODE_BLK].
- [SEG_GRAN] composes with [SEG_BINN] under [BLK_PATN] to form a [NODE_BLK].

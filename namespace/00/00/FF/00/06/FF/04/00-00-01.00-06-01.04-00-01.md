```
node_id: 00-00-01.00-06-01.04-00-01
node_nam: Node Identifier Segment
node_tag: NODE_SEG
```

## Definition

[NODE_SEG] is one two-digit hexadecimal component of a [NODE_ID].

Each [NODE_SEG] corresponds to one position in the identifier hierarchy. Nine [NODE_SEG] are concatenated with dot separators to form a full [NODE_ID].

[NODE_SEG] values range from `00` to `FF`. The value `00` denotes the identity position at its position. The value `FF` is reserved.

## Rules

- A [NODE_SEG] MAY be constrained by its position document to a controlled vocabulary of assignable values.
- For an unconstrained position, the authority governing the parent scope assigns non-zero values.
- Assignments are unique within one parent scope at that position and MAY be sparse.
- Once a non-zero [NODE_SEG] value is assigned within one parent scope at one position, it MUST NOT be reassigned within that same scope to identify a different [FOLIO].
- A [NODE_SEG] is syntactically valid in canonical uppercase form; parsers MUST normalize lowercase to uppercase.

## Constraints

- Each [NODE_SEG] is exactly two hexadecimal digits (00–FF in canonical uppercase).
- Values `01` to `FE` are assignable unless the position document further constrains them.
- `00` is the identity value at any position and is not an assignable non-identity value.
- `FF` is reserved and must not appear in any assigned [NODE_ID].
- [NODE_SEG] composition is immutable once committed to a [FOLIO].

## Relations

- [NODE_SEG] is a component of [NODE_ID].
- Each [NODE_SEG] corresponds to exactly one position.
- [NODE_SEG] values are ordered (position 1–9 within a [NODE_ID]) and position determines position meaning.
- Nine [NODE_SEG] concatenated with dot separators form one [NODE_ID].
- position documents define constraints on assignable [NODE_SEG] values at their position.

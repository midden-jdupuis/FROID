## Definition

[BLK_COMP] is the role a [NODE_BLK] plays as a unit of [NODE_ID] composition. A [NODE_ID] is an ordered sequence of [NODE_BLK]; the count of blocks and the segment functions within each block are determined by the [FROID_NS] projection in effect.

## Rules

- A [NODE_ID] is composed of an ordered sequence of [NODE_BLK].
- The count of [NODE_BLK] in a [NODE_ID] is fixed at [FROID_NS] instantiation and recorded in the [FROID_NS] root [FOLIO]. Different projections MAY declare different block counts.
- The first [SEG_GRAN] segment of block 1 (VV) is the company segment, identifying the entity that owns the [NODE_ID] space.
- The first [SEG_GRAN] segment of block 2 (RR, OID position 4) is the [PROJECT] instantiation selector. Its value selects which [PROJECT] is in effect for the remainder of the [NODE_ID] under the company-branch.
- All [NODE_SEG] other than VV (position 1, company) and RR (position 4, instantiation) receive their function from the [PROJECT] in effect. The framework specifies the abstract block pattern and these two framework-level positions; remaining segment functions are [PROJECT]-specific.
- Block specifications MAY be portable across projections (e.g., a mode block, a type block) or domain-specific (e.g., a unit/callable block). Portability is a property of the block specification, not of the framework's composition rule.

## Constraints

- A [NODE_ID] MUST contain at least one [NODE_BLK]. A zero-block [NODE_ID] is not addressable.
- The framework-level segments (VV at position 1, RR at position 4) are normative across all [PROJECT] instantiations. A [PROJECT] MAY NOT redefine their roles.
- The current [FROID_NS] uses three [NODE_BLK] of three segments each: Block 1 (WHO) = (VV, BB, MM); Block 2 (WHAT NAMESPACE) = (RR, CC, TT); Block 3 (WHAT ELEMENT) = (UU, KK, II). Future [PROJECT] instantiations (legal, marketing, etc.) MAY declare different counts.

## Relations

- [BLK_COMP] is a facet of [NODE_BLK].
- [BLK_COMP] determines [NODE_ID] structure given a projection.
- [BLK_COMP] interacts with [FROID_NS] root [FOLIO] (which records block count for the namespace).

## Notes

- The framework intentionally specifies only the block pattern, the company role at position 1, and the instantiation role at position 4. All other segment semantics are [PROJECT]-level concerns. This factoring keeps the framework portable across decomposition domains.
- A future framework-level prefix block (FROID/USERID/LICENSE) above any [FROID_NS] is reserved for v2.0+ to bridge global X.660 addressing and per-tenant namespaces. v1.0 keeps the existing `1.3.6.1.4.1` X.660 export and does not materialize the prefix block.

## Definition

[BLK_SCPE] is the role a [NODE_BLK] plays as a boundary for local rules, authority, naming, and supersession. Within a [NODE_BLK] scope, governance MAY apply locally without propagating to peer or ancestor scopes.

## Rules

- A [NODE_BLK] defines a scope. References, rules, and authority MAY apply within that scope without propagating outside it.
- A user MAY be granted authority over a [NODE_BLK] scope without being granted authority over peer or ancestor [NODE_BLK] scopes.
- A [NODE_TAG] MAY be defined within a [NODE_BLK] scope without conflicting with same-named tags in peer [NODE_BLK] scopes.
- A supersession event MAY apply at [NODE_BLK] scope rather than per-[FOLIO].

## Constraints

- The scoping capabilities listed above are structurally available at v1.0; their operational governance (e.g., authority delegation rules, supersession-at-scope semantics) is reserved for the authority operationalization round.

## Relations

- [BLK_SCPE] is a facet of [NODE_BLK].
- [BLK_SCPE] interacts with [FLIO_AUT] (block-scoped authority is independent of per-[FOLIO] authority).
- [BLK_SCPE] interacts with [FLIO_REL] (supersession MAY operate at block scope or [FOLIO] scope).

## Notes

- Block-scoped authority and use-class governance compose along independent dimensions: a user holding authority over a [NODE_BLK] scope and a use-class declared on that same scope are governed by separate rules. The composition rule is a deferred future-seam for v1.0.
- Recognizing [NODE_BLK] as a scoping unit (not just a structural grouping) makes capabilities like block-scoped authority explicit. These capabilities were structurally available before this recognition; the framing makes them addressable.

## Definition

[BLK_PATN] is the structural shape of every [NODE_BLK]: an ordered sequence of [SEG_GRAN] segments terminated by exactly one [SEG_BINN] segment. The pattern is uniform across all blocks at all levels in any [FROID_NS] projection.

## Rules

- Every [NODE_BLK] MUST consist of [SEG_GRAN] segments followed by exactly one [SEG_BINN] segment.
- The [SEG_BINN] segment MUST occupy the last position of the [NODE_BLK].
- The pattern is derived, not asserted: a field is terminal and admits no sub-resolution, so the segment that classifies the field MUST terminate the block; the segments preceding it MUST locate the document on which the field appears.
- The pattern applies at every [NODE_BLK] in every [FROID_NS] projection. The number of [SEG_GRAN] segments per block and the number of blocks per [NODE_ID] are projection-specific; the pattern itself is framework-level.

## Constraints

- A [NODE_BLK] MAY NOT contain zero [SEG_GRAN] segments — at least one locating segment is required for a block to resolve a document.
- A [NODE_BLK] MAY NOT contain more than one [SEG_BINN] segment — field-terminality admits exactly one terminating segment per block.

## Relations

- [BLK_PATN] is a facet of [NODE_BLK].
- [BLK_PATN] constrains the legal arrangement of [SEG_GRAN] and [SEG_BINN] within a [NODE_BLK].

## Notes

- The current [FROID_NS] instantiates the pattern as two-granular-one-bin per block across three blocks: Block 1 (WHO), Block 2 (WHAT NAMESPACE), Block 3 (WHAT ELEMENT). Other [PROJECT] instantiations MAY use more locating segments per block; the framework does not constrain the count.

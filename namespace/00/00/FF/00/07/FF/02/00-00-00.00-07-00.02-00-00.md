## Definition

[PROJ_TIM] is the [PROJECT] that encodes Unix-epoch moments as [NODE_ID]. A [PROJ_TIM] [NODE_ID] addresses a single second-resolution moment. The 32-bit Unix-epoch integer is split into four hex bytes (most-significant byte first) and laid out across blocks 3 and 4; sign is carried separately in a [SEG_BINN] segment.

## Rules

- [PROJ_TIM] declares 4 [NODE_BLK]:
  - Block 1 (WHO): VV / BB / MM (framework-fixed).
  - Block 2 (WHAT NAMESPACE): RR / CC / TT — RR is tenant-allocated; CC=02 ([PROJ_TIM]'s catalog-row id); TT classifies against [TYPE] and is 00 (identity) for v1.0 [PROJ_TIM] moment folios.
  - Block 3: aa / bb / BC — aa = epoch byte 1 (most significant); bb = epoch byte 2; BC ([SEG_BINN]) = epoch sign.
  - Block 4: cc / dd / DE — cc = epoch byte 3; dd = epoch byte 4 (least significant); DE ([SEG_BINN]) = reserved 00.
- The Unix-epoch integer is encoded as absolute magnitude. aa/bb/cc/dd carry the magnitude in MSB-first byte order; sign is carried by BC.
- BC bin values: 01 = post-1970 (epoch ≥ 0); 02 = pre-1970 (epoch < 0). A positive epoch and the corresponding negative epoch of equal absolute value share the same aa/bb/cc/dd grans and are distinguished by BC alone.
- DE bin is reserved 00 in [PROJ_TIM]. v1.0 has no defined classification axis on DE.
- A [PROJ_TIM] folio materializes only at the resolution actually addressed (one second). [PROJ_TIM] moments are sparse on disk; the path's FF directories are structural aggregators, not folios.

## Constraints

- [PROJ_TIM] commits to 32-bit Unix epoch. The encoding has a forward horizon at year 2106 (post-1970, unsigned magnitude overflow at 0xFFFFFFFF) and a symmetric backward horizon (pre-1970). Sub-second resolution and finer-than-second time addresses are out of scope for [PROJ_TIM].
- TT must classify against [TYPE]. v1.0 [PROJ_TIM] uses TT=00 (identity); revisit when the TT-to-[TYPE]-entry wiring is formalized.

## Relations

- [PROJ_TIM] is a child of [PROJECT].
- [PROJ_TIM] is applied wherever an (VV, BB) tree assigns RR to a [PROJ_TIM] instance; the applied [NODE_ID] carries CC=02.

## Notes

- Worked example. Epoch 1777987384 (= 0x69F9EF38) under FROID, Inc. at RR=01:
  - NODE_ID: `01-01-00.01-02-00.69-F9-01.EF-38-00`
  - Path: `/namespace/01/01/FF/01/02/FF/69/F9/FF/EF/38/01-01-00.01-02-00.69-F9-01.EF-38-00.frd`
- A [PROJECT] requiring sub-second resolution is authored as a separate [PROJECT] catalog entry (a new CC value) — **not** as an extension of [PROJ_TIM]. Extension between [PROJECT] is reserved for prepend-scope (see [PROJECT] Notes); a refinement of time resolution is a different shape, not an outer scope. [PROJ_TIM] reserves no structural extension points; the DE bin remains reserved 00 and is not a hook.
- The folio body schema for [PROJ_TIM] moments is open in v1.0. [PROJ_TIM] specifies addressing only; the content of a moment-folio is left freeform until use cases drive a schema.
